Gluten Free Diet

A gluten-free diet involves avoiding foods that contain gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and their derivatives. This diet is essential for individuals with celiac disease, a serious autoimmune disorder triggered by gluten ingestion, causing damage to the small intestine. Gluten sensitivity or wheat allergy can also prompt individuals to adopt a gluten-free lifestyle. Common gluten-free foods include fruits, vegetables, meat, fish, eggs, dairy, and gluten-free grains like rice and quinoa. Gluten-free alternatives, such as rice flour or almond flour, replace traditional wheat-based products. While crucial for those with gluten-related disorders, a gluten-free diet may lack certain nutrients, so careful planning is necessary. It has gained popularity beyond medical necessity, with some people attributing benefits like improved digestion and increased energy to gluten avoidance. However, there's limited scientific evidence supporting these claims for individuals without gluten-related disorders. Consulting with a healthcare professional or a registered dietitian is advisable before adopting a gluten-free diet.
